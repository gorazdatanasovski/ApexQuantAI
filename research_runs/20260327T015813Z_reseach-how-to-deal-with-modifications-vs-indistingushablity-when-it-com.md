## Statement
Assume log-volatility X is generated by a Volterra kernel K with local singularity K(t,s) ~ c (t-s)^{H-1/2} for H in (0,1/2). Then the q-variation and increment variance of X at fine scales obey a power law whose slope identifies H, and realized short-horizon volatility inherits the same roughness exponent up to lower-order microstructure distortion.

## Assumptions
- X admits a Volterra representation with kernel singularity exponent H-1/2.
- Observed prices admit a sufficiently fine daily or intraday volatility proxy.
- Microstructure noise is lower order on the estimation scale or explicitly corrected.
- Empirical scope anchored to SPY US Equity.

## Variables
- **H**: roughness / Hurst-type exponent
- **K**: Volterra kernel
- **X_t**: latent log-volatility process
- **RV_{t,Δ}**: realized variance proxy over scale Δ
